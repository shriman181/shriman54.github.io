The Kognos Platform

Integrated intelligence for pest and vector operations at scale

Kognos combines environmental sensing, historical outbreak data, and machine learning to deliver a complete operational intelligence platform — not another dashboard of disconnected charts.

How It Works

From raw signal
to field decision.

Kognos ingests multi-source data, runs continuous predictive models, and surfaces decision-ready outputs — all within a single operator interface.

01

Data Ingestion

Kognos pulls from weather APIs, satellite imagery (NDVI, thermal), field sensor networks, historical infestation databases, and crop management records. No manual data entry required.

02

Predictive Modelling

LSTM and ensemble ML models trained on pest phenology, climate variables, and crop growth stages run continuously — updating outbreak probability surfaces every 6 hours.

03

Risk Surface Generation

Geospatial risk maps overlay outbreak probability with field geometry — highlighting hotspots, tracking resistance clusters, and flagging vector breeding corridors in real time.

04

Operational Dispatch

Optimised application routes are pushed directly to field teams — with prioritised zones, recommended compounds (resistance-aware), compliance logging, and outcome tracking built in.

05

Feedback & Learning

Field outcomes are captured and fed back into model training — so every season, every treated outbreak, and every resistance report makes the system sharper for your specific geography.

Platform Modules

Every module built
for operational reality.

Not lab research. Not academic models. Kognos modules are designed around the actual decision cycles of field managers, PCO coordinators, and public health officials.

MODULE 01

Outbreak Forecasting

Agricultural

Predictive outbreak models for 40+ key agricultural pest species — including aphids, whitefly, armyworm, locust pressure, and fungal vectors like Botrytis and Fusarium. Models integrate pest development stage (degree-day accumulation), host crop phenology, neighbouring field infestation reports, and real-time climate data.

Output: 48–72 hour outbreak probability maps, alert thresholds calibrated to economic injury levels, and recommended intervention windows. Explainable AI features surface which signals drove the forecast — not a black box.

MODULE 02

Vector Risk Mapping

Municipal

Disease vector risk maps for mosquito-borne pathogens (Dengue, Malaria, Zika, Chikungunya, West Nile Virus). Combines standing water detection from satellite thermal/optical data, temperature-dependent vector population models, larval survey integration, and historical disease incidence records.

Delivered as a GIS-native interface municipalities can layer onto existing city planning systems. Outbreak risk updates daily; fogging and larviciding priority zones are generated automatically ahead of outbreak windows.

MODULE 03

Application Routing

Operations

Dynamic route optimisation for pesticide application teams — prioritising highest-risk zones, minimising travel time, factoring wind and temperature application windows, and preventing over-treatment of already-declining pest populations. Routes update as new field data comes in.

Compliance logging built in: application volumes, compound used, GPS-stamped coverage maps, and technician sign-off are captured automatically — reducing audit burden and strengthening regulatory compliance records.

MODULE 04

Resistance Tracking

Intelligence

A continuously updated regional resistance database, built from field treatment outcome reports, bioassay data submissions, and published genomic resistance markers. Identifies which active compounds are losing efficacy — and where resistance is spreading geographically.

Distributors receive compound efficacy scores by region, enabling smarter product recommendations. Agronomists receive IRM (Integrated Resistance Management) protocol suggestions aligned with actual regional resistance profiles.

About Kognos

The intelligence layer

Why Kognos exists

Pest management is one of the oldest agricultural challenges — and one of the most technologically neglected. The industry has GPS-guided tractors, precision irrigation, and yield forecasting models. But when it comes to pests, the baseline tool is still a spray calendar printed in January.

The data to do better has existed for years. Climate APIs, satellite multispectral imagery, IoT field sensors, genomic resistance databases — these are all available. What didn't exist was a B2B platform that integrated these signals into a usable operational workflow for the people actually managing pest control at scale.

That's the gap Kognos was built to close.

  • We operate in the space between raw data and field decision — not one or the other.
  • We build for operators first: agronomists, municipal health coordinators, PCO field managers.
  • We believe resistance management is a collective good — our network effect is strongest when data is shared.
  • We measure success in seasons saved, not dashboards launched.

The market context

Pests destroy up to 40% of global crop production annually — equivalent to $220 billion in losses. Mosquito-borne disease costs emerging economies an additional $12 billion per year in public health spending and lost productivity.

The AI-based pest management market was valued at $2.5 billion in 2024 and is forecast to reach $8.4 billion by 2032. But the majority of that market is in reactive detection tools — sensors, cameras, identification apps. The predictive layer — knowing where and when before the outbreak — remains largely unaddressed.

Kognos is positioned at the predictive edge of this market: not detection, but prevention. Not monitoring, but forecasting. This is a greenfield B2B SaaS category with no dominant player.
